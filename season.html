The Calendar

Foraging Through the Seasons

Spring (Feb–Apr)

Morels emerge in the Western Ghats. St. George's mushrooms in grasslands. Excellent for wood ears.

MorelWood Ear

Summer (May–Jul)

Pre-monsoon species after first rains. Porcini in higher elevations. Chicken of the Woods on fallen oaks.

Chicken of Woods

Monsoon (Aug–Sep)

Peak season! Chanterelles, oyster mushrooms, Reishi and Lion's Mane are most abundant in forests now.

ChanterelleReishi

Autumn (Oct–Jan)

Late boletes and hedgehog mushrooms. Beefsteak fungus in abundance. Coastal foraging begins.

BoleteBeefsteak

🌿 Pro Tip

The best time to forage is 2–3 days after rain, when temperatures are 15–22°C. Early morning gives the freshest finds before afternoon heat sets in.

FAQs

Foraging Frequently Asked Questions

Is foraging safe for beginners?

Yes, when done under expert guidance. We never consume any mushroom without 100% positive identification by two trained guides. Beginner tours focus on species with no dangerous lookalikes.

What should I bring on a foraging tour?

Sturdy closed-toe shoes, long pants, a hat. We provide baskets, ID cards, and gloves. Bring water, snacks, and a notebook for sketching. A smartphone for photos is highly recommended.

Can children join the tours?

Children aged 8+ are welcome on beginner tours with a parent or guardian. Family-friendly routes with simplified identification activities that children absolutely love.

What is the cancellation policy?

Full refund for cancellations 7+ days before. 50% refund within 48 hours. Tours cancelled by ForageWild due to weather receive full refunds or free rescheduling.

Do you offer private group bookings?

Yes! Custom private tours for groups of 6–20 people. Corporate team building, birthday forays, and school trips available. Contact us for pricing.
