Safety First

Essential Safety Guidelines

🚫 Never Do This
  • Eat any unidentified mushroom
  • Forage alone in unfamiliar areas
  • Touch eyes after handling fungi
  • Rely solely on apps for ID Proof
⚠️ Always Verify
  • Check cap, gills, stem and spore
  • Cross-reference multiple guides
  • Know all dangerous lookalikes
  • Test small amounts spore print
✅ Best Practices
  • Carry a physical field guide
  • Wear gloves when handling
  • Use mesh bags for spore spread
  • Photograph before picking
🚑 Emergency Info
  • Poison Control: 1800-116-117
  • Save sample of mushroom eaten
  • Seek medical help immediately
  • time and amount consumed
⚠️ Know These

Deadly Species to Avoid

Memorise these species before every outing. Identification could save your life.

💀 Deadly

Death Cap

Amanita phalloides. Pale green/white cap. White gills. Ring on stem. Volva at base. Causes 90% of fatal mushroom poisonings.

⚡ Lethal

Destroying Angel

Amanita virosa. Pure white all over. Often mistaken for edible button mushrooms. Contains amatoxins — no antidote exists.

⚠️ Toxic

Fly Agaric

Amanita muscaria. Iconic red cap with white spots. Psychoactive and toxic. Causes delirium, vomiting and serious organ stress.

⚠️ Dangerous

False Morel

Gyromitra esculenta. Brain-like wrinkled cap. Easily confused with true morels. Contains gyromitrin — converts to toxic hydrazine.

First Aid

If Accidental Ingestion Occurs

Act fast. Every minute matters when dealing with toxic mushroom consumption. Follow these steps immediately.

1

Do Not Induce Vomiting

Unless directed by a medical professional. Vomiting can cause additional harm in some cases of poisoning.

2

Save the Specimen

Keep the remaining mushroom or photograph it. Bring it to the hospital — it helps doctors identify the toxin and plan treatment.

3

Call Poison Control

India Poison Control: 1800-116-117 (toll-free, 24/7). Seek hospital emergency care immediately.

4

Note Time & Amount

Record when the mushroom was eaten and how much. This information is critical for medical staff.
